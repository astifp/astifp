Join us for an exciting evening exploring the intersection of economics and diplomacy. Diplonomics brings together leading experts in international trade, foreign policy, and economic development to discuss how economic principles shape diplomatic relations and vice versa.

üìç

Location

Ashoka University

Master the art of crafting compelling policy briefs in this hands-on workshop designed for students interested in policy research and advocacy. Learn from experienced policy analysts about the structure, style, and strategies for writing impactful policy recommendations.

Guest Lecture: Trade Wars & Global Tariffs

üìÖ

Date

November 2, 2024

5:00 PM - 6:30 PM

üìç

Location

Ashoka University

Trivedi Auditorium

Join us for an illuminating lecture by Dr. Rajesh Mehta, former Economic Advisor to the Ministry of Commerce, as he analyzes the current state of global trade tensions and their implications for emerging economies. Drawing from decades of experience in trade negotiations, Dr. Mehta will provide insider perspectives on the complexities of international trade policy.


The lecture will examine recent trade disputes, the future of multilateral trade agreements, and strategies for developing countries to navigate an increasingly protectionist global environment. A Q&A session will follow, offering students a unique opportunity to engage with a leading expert on issues that are reshaping the global economic order.

Model UN Conference 2024

üìÖ

Date

November 15-17, 2024

9:00 AM - 6:00 PM (Daily)

üìç

Location

Ashoka University

Multiple Venues on Campus

Experience the thrill of international diplomacy at our annual Model UN Conference! This three-day simulation brings together students from across universities to represent different countries, debate pressing global issues, and negotiate resolutions in various UN committees.


This year's agenda includes committees on international trade and development, climate change negotiations, cybersecurity governance, and humanitarian crises. Participants will develop skills in public speaking, negotiation, research, and diplomatic protocol while gaining deeper understanding of how international organizations function. Awards will be presented for outstanding delegates in each committee. Registration is open to all university students with an interest in international affairs.
